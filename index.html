Siyu Wang

I am Siyu Wang and I'm now a 6th year PhD student from the Program of Computational Biology and Bioinformatics at Duke University. I'm also affiliated to the Department of Computer Science at Duke University.

My PhD thesis advisor is Prof. Bruce R. Donald, who is a James B. Duke professor of Computer Science, Mathematics, Chemistry and Biochemistry at Duke University, and also an ACM fellow and IEEE fellow. In Prof. Donald's lab, we work on developing computational protein design algorithm and software, and apply them to real biological systems (most of which are human health related). I am specifically working on a few projects including designing novel therapeutics to cystic fibrosis, methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA), and human immunodeficiency virus (HIV).

Before joining Duke, I received my bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University at China in 2015, majored in control and electrical engineering. When I was there, I worked closely with Prof. Jianyang Zeng, who is an associate professor at Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences (IIIS) at Tsinghua University. We worked together on developing novel computational method to model the 3D structure of chromatin, using chromosome conformation capture (3C) data and Hi-C data.

In my leisure time I like to play video games. I am a huge fan of Dota 2, and also Dota Auto Chess.
